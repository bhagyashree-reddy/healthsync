News and Announcements

Indian Republic Day (January 26)

Indian Republic Day commemorates the adoption of the Indian Constitution in 1950, transforming India into a republic. Schools celebrate with vibrant parades, flag hoisting ceremonies, and cultural programs, highlighting the nation's unity and diverse heritage. It's a day of pride and patriotism, reflecting on the country's democratic values.

Indian Independence Day(Aug 15)

Independence Day marks India's freedom from British rule in 1947. Schools honor this significant occasion with flag hoisting, speeches, and performances that celebrate India's journey to freedom. It's a day of national pride, reflecting on the sacrifices made for the country's sovereignty and independence..

Children's Day (November 14)

Children's Day is celebrated on November 14, the birthday of Jawaharlal Nehru, India's first Prime Minister, who was known for his love of children. Schools organize special events and activities to recognize and celebrate the importance of children in society. It's a day dedicated to the well-being and development of children, emphasizing their role as the future of the nation.
